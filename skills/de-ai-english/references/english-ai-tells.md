# English AI Tells — taxonomy

The smell is statistical, not lexical. These are the patterns LLMs overproduce. Diagnose the 3–5 most damaging in a given draft; don't flag all.

## Rhythm & structure
- **Uniform sentence length.** Every sentence 12–18 words. Humans spike: a 4-word line next to a 30-word one.
- **Rule-of-three.** "plan, debate, and execute." "faster, cheaper, and simpler." Three balanced items is the model's default cadence. Use one, or two, or four.
- **Perfect symmetry / parallelism.** "It scales when you grow, and grows when you scale." Reads engineered.
- **Listicle reflex.** Turning any idea into a bulleted set with emoji headers when prose would do.
- **Tidy completeness.** Intro → three points → "In conclusion." Real writing trails off, jumps, leaves edges.

## Sentence-level constructions
- **Em-dash drama.** "The data is the real story — and everyone missed it." LLMs lean on the em-dash for false weight. Prefer a period.
- **"It's not X, it's Y."** "It's not a tool, it's a teammate." "This isn't about speed, it's about trust." A signature tell. Use sparingly and only when literally true.
- **"While X, Y" fake balance.** "While the results are promising, challenges remain." Hedging dressed as nuance.
- **Negation pivot.** "It's less about the code and more about the people." Same family as "not X, it's Y."
- **Forced escalation.** "Not only faster, but smarter." "More than a product — a movement."

## Openers & fillers
- "Honestly," "Look," "Here's the thing," "The truth is," "Let's be real," "Turns out,"
- "In today's fast-paced world," "In the age of AI,"
- Restating the question/post before answering it.

## Diction
- Buzzwords: leverage, unlock, delve, robust, seamless, game-changer, supercharge, harness, elevate, streamline, empower, foster, navigate (figurative), tapestry, realm, landscape (figurative), moat (unless quoting).
- Hedge stack: "can potentially help," "may sometimes," "tends to often."
- Empty intensifiers: "truly," "incredibly," "remarkably," "deeply."

## Stance & substance
- **No author position.** Describes both sides, commits to neither. Humans have an opinion and a reason.
- **No concrete subject.** "Teams," "businesses," "users" instead of a named person, tool, or moment.
- **No evidence anchor.** Big claims with no number, example, or lived detail. (Fix by narrowing, never by inventing.)
- **Safe neutral tone.** Agreeable, polished, risk-free. Real voices have edges and blunt moments.
- **Summarize-then-add.** In replies: paraphrasing the other person's point back to them before making yours.

## Platform-specific
- **X / social reply:** AI replies open by validating ("Great point!"), then add a tidy paragraph. Humans cut to the point, fragment, sometimes start lowercase.
- **Cold email:** AI opens with flattery + "I came across your..." Humans state why they're writing in line one.
- **Landing page:** AI writes balanced feature triplets and "Empower your team to..." Humans lead with one sharp claim and a real outcome.
