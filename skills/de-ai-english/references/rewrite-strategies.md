# Rewrite Strategies

Operations to apply after diagnosis. Goal: change the distribution, not swap synonyms.

## 1. Break the rhythm
Force variance. Put a short line (3–6 words) next to a long one. Read it aloud; if every sentence has the same length and shape, rewrite until they don't.

## 2. Restore a concrete subject
Replace "teams / businesses / users" with a named person, tool, number, or moment the draft already implies. "Users churn" → "the legacy cohort kept paying for two years." If no concrete detail exists, narrow the claim — never fabricate one.

## 3. Kill symmetry
Find balanced pairs and triplets. Cut one element, or make them uneven. "plan, debate, and execute" → "they argue, then ship." Asymmetry reads human.

## 4. Delete the em-dash drama
Replace "X — the real Y" with two sentences, or fold it in plainly. Reserve em-dashes for genuine asides, at most one per piece.

## 5. Dismantle "not X, it's Y"
Rewrite as a direct claim. "It's not a tool, it's a teammate" → "It works like a teammate: it pushes back when I'm wrong." Keep the construction only when the contrast is literally the point and used once.

## 6. Give the author a position
Add one real opinion with a reason and a boundary. Not "AI has pros and cons" but "I'd give an agent the inbox, not the contract." Commitment is the most human signal.

## 7. Cut the opener filler
Delete "Honestly," "Here's the thing," "The truth is." Start on the actual point. In replies, drop the summary of what the other person said.

## 8. Plain-word pass
Swap buzzwords for plain verbs: leverage→use, unlock→get, streamline→simplify, empower→let, foster→build. Remove empty intensifiers (truly, incredibly, remarkably).

## 9. Let it be imperfect
Allow a fragment. A lowercase start. A blunt, slightly unbalanced sentence. One aside in parentheses. Real people typing fast aren't tidy. Don't overdo it into fake-casual.

## 10. Fix the ending
Cut "In conclusion / At the end of the day / Ultimately." End on the last useful thing, or a real question, or just stop.

## Depth levels
- **Light:** tells 1, 4, 7, 8 only. Keep structure. For copy that's mostly fine.
- **Standard:** all ten, one pass, preserve meaning and length.
- **Heavy:** rebuild rhythm and stance from scratch while preserving facts and coverage. For copy that reads fully machine-written.

## What not to do
- Don't fabricate detail to sound specific.
- Don't force first person or spoken markers into genres that reject them (technical, legal, formal).
- Don't wash human-written passages in a mixed draft — edit locally.
- Don't chase a detector score.
